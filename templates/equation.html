How it works

            

                {% if slug == "pythagorean-theorem" %}
                
In any right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle) equals the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

                
c² = a² + b²  →  c = √(a² + b²)

                
This is the foundation of Euclidean geometry and is used in construction, navigation, and physics.


                {% elif slug == "quadratic-formula" %}
                
A quadratic equation ax² + bx + c = 0 has solutions given by:

                
x = (-b ± √(b²-4ac)) / 2a

                
The discriminant (b²-4ac) determines the nature of the roots: positive → two real roots, zero → one repeated root, negative → complex roots.


                {% elif slug == "area-of-circle" %}
                
The area of a circle is proportional to the square of its radius.

                
A = π × r²

                
where π ≈ 3.14159 is the mathematical constant representing the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.


                {% elif slug == "volume-of-sphere" %}
                
The volume of a sphere depends on the cube of its radius:

                
V = (4/3) × π × r³

                
This formula was discovered by Archimedes around 250 BC.


                {% elif slug == "area-of-triangle" %}
                
The area of a triangle is half the product of its base and perpendicular height:

                
A = ½ × base × height

                
This works regardless of the triangle's shape — the height must be measured perpendicular to the base.


                {% elif slug == "linear-equation" %}
                
A straight line is described by the slope-intercept form:

                
y = mx + c

                
m is the slope (rate of change) and c is the y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis).


                {% elif slug == "factorial" %}
                
The factorial of a non-negative integer n is the product of all positive integers up to n:

                
n! = n × (n-1) × (n-2) × ... × 2 × 1

                
By definition, 0! = 1. Factorials grow extremely fast (10! = 3,628,800).


                {% elif slug == "combination" %}
                
A combination counts how many ways to choose r items from n where order doesn't matter:

                
C(n, r) = n! / (r! × (n-r)!)

                
Example: choosing 2 cards from a deck of 52 → C(52,2) = 1326.


                {% elif slug == "permutation" %}
                
A permutation counts how many ways to arrange r items from n where order matters:

                
P(n, r) = n! / (n-r)!

                
P(n, r) is always ≥ C(n, r) because every combination can be rearranged in r! ways.


                {% elif slug == "sine-rule" %}
                
The Sine Rule relates sides and angles in any triangle:

                
a/sin(A) = b/sin(B) = c/sin(C)

                
It's especially useful when you know one side and two angles, or two sides and a non-included angle.


                {% elif slug == "cosine-rule" %}
                
The Cosine Rule generalises the Pythagorean theorem for any triangle:

                
c² = a² + b² - 2ab × cos(C)

                
When C = 90°, cos(C) = 0, and the formula reduces to c² = a² + b².


                {% elif slug == "sine-value" %}
                
The sine function relates an angle in a right triangle to the ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse:

                
sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse

                
It oscillates between -1 and 1 and is fundamental in wave mechanics and signal processing.


                {% elif slug == "cosine-value" %}
                
The cosine function relates an angle to the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse:

                
cos(θ) = adjacent / hypotenuse

                
It is the sine function shifted by 90°: cos(θ) = sin(θ + 90°).


                {% elif slug == "tangent-value" %}
                
The tangent function is the ratio of sine to cosine:

                
tan(θ) = sin(θ) / cos(θ) = opposite / adjacent

                
tan(θ) is undefined when cos(θ) = 0 (i.e., at 90° and 270°).


                {% elif slug == "newtons-second-law" %}
                
Newton's Second Law of Motion states that force is proportional to mass and acceleration:

                
F = m × a

                
where F is in Newtons, m in kilograms, and a in m/s². This is the cornerstone of classical mechanics.


                {% elif slug == "kinetic-energy" %}
                
Kinetic energy is the energy an object possesses due to its motion:

                
KE = ½ × m × v²

                
If you double the velocity, kinetic energy quadruples (because of the v² term).


                {% elif slug == "potential-energy" %}
                
Gravitational potential energy is the energy stored by an object at a height above a reference point:

                
PE = m × g × h

                
On Earth, g ≈ 9.81 m/s². PE is converted to kinetic energy as the object falls.


                {% elif slug == "ohms-law" %}
                
Ohm's Law relates voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit:

                
V = I × R

                
Provide any two values to solve for the third. V in volts, I in amps, R in ohms.


                {% elif slug == "mean" %}
                
The arithmetic mean (average) sums all values and divides by the count:

                
x̄ = (x₁ + x₂ + ... + xₙ) / n

                
Enter values separated by commas.


                {% elif slug == "standard-deviation" %}
                
The population standard deviation measures how spread out values are from the mean:

                
σ = √( Σ(xᵢ - x̄)² / n )

                
A low standard deviation means data points are close to the mean; a high value indicates spread.


                {% elif slug == "median" %}
                
The median is the middle value of a sorted dataset. If the dataset has an even number of values, the median is the average of the two middle values.

                
Unlike the mean, the median is not affected by outliers.

                {% endif %}
